>[!info]
>These are the rules we used for a game of Hide & Seek around Cardiff. Please feel free to use them as a bouncing off point for your own game.
## The Basics
* We are using the **Medium** size ruleset.
	* Hiding time is set at **1 Hour**.
	* Use the **M** durations on the cards.
	* The Hiding Zone encompasses a circle spanning **500 metres** from the transit station.
* The game map is defined as all stations covered by the **Explore Cardiff and Valleys ticket / The Valley Lines Day Explorer**.
	* Map: [tfw.wales](https://tfw.wales/sites/default/files/2018-10/Valley%20Explorer%20LG_1.pdf)
* The order of play will be determined by the drawing of straws.
* The initial hiding period begins at 09:30 on Saturday 6th September, directly outside Cardiff Central station. Given a hiding period of 1 Hour, this means that the Seekers shall be released (and the hiding clock will start) at 10:30.
	* All participants need to purchase the appropriate rover from the ticket office at Cardiff Central before the initial hiding period (this rover can only be purchased from ticket offices, frustratingly).
* Timekeeping is the shared responsibility of the Seekers (though the hider may also wish to keep a stopwatch running).
* The rest period shall begin at 19:30 (after 9 hours of play time). All participants shall return to Cardiff Central station for 21:00 at the latest. The hider may choose to voluntarily delay their arrival to obscure their chosen hiding spot.
	* The rest period ends at 10:30 the following day.
* For the avoidance of doubt, the only permissible transit stations to hide from are National Rail / Transport for Wales rail stations.
* All participants are expected to have at least one hiding period each. Once all participants successfully hide once, the hiding role shall loop with the same order as the first round until the end of the game, unless that person has no possibility of improving their score with the time remaining, in which case it shall rotate to the next person (or discretion shall be used).
* The game will end at 19:30 on Sunday 7th September - whomever is presently hiding will stop their hiding clock at this time. The winner is the person with the longest continuous hiding period.
## Additional Rules
* **If an endgame is in progress on Sunday the 7th at 2:58pm, the game will be paused for 5 minutes (until 3:03pm).** This is for the national Emergency Alerts test.
* **Transit rules:**
	* **Transit via Newport is not permitted.**
	* **GWR services are not permitted.** This includes fast services via Bridgend or Newport. Only TfW services are allowed.
	* Buses:
		* Usage of rail-replacement buses (including on the closed Rhymney / Coryton branches) is permitted. This includes bus services that replace the rail service, as described on the improvement works page: [tfw.wales](https://tfw.wales/service-status/planned-improvement-works)
		* Hiders are not permitted to use buses, **except to connect between the termini of the Heads of the Valley** (Treherbert, Aberdare, Merthyr Tidfil, Rhymney, and Ebbw Vale).
			* Bus stops are not valid hiding points - only rail stations.
			* It is the responsibility of the hider to ensure they arrive at any rail station before the end of the hiding period. Failure to reach a station before the end of the hiding period, and subsequent failure to answer a question, is handled in the same way as a normal failed question would be (stopped clock, forfeit card draw).
			* This rule is in place to avoid the game potentially becoming jammed on a single valley.
		* Seekers may use the above Heads of the Valley buses, but **other buses may only be used to avoid severe transit network disruption.**
		* **Stagecoach South Wales & Thomas of Rhondda buses are included in the Rover ticket.**
	* Walking is, of course, permitted. It is explicitly permissible to walk to perform an OSI (for example, Cardiff Central <-> Cardiff Queen Street).
* Matching questions involving Zones (e.g "Zone starts with Same Letter") are based on the OSM Zone 10 name.
* In case of severe transit network disruption, the game may either be temporarily paused or, in the worst case, abandoned, if all participants agree.
## Personal Safety
* **Personal safety is paramount**: if anyone has any concerns, they should be raised as soon as possible with a view to immediate mitigation.
	* If the hider is concerned for their personal safety for any reason during the endgame, movement is obviously permitted - the game will be frozen and the clock will stop. The game will resume once everyone is satisfied that it is safe to do so. If not, the game shall be abandoned.
* Both sides should be carrying basic first aid (plasters, etc)
* Carry a water bottle at all times, and refill at every opportunity (especially if a changeover is likely soon)
