#recipe #recipe/advanced

*Fed up with Orange Chicken? Behold, this one might float your boat even more.*
*A little note though: if you're not a fan of fishy tastes / strong umami, this might not be for you - it does use quite a bit of fish sauce.*

> [!warning]
> Always wash your hands after handling raw meat, along with any surfaces it touches - unless you enjoy food poisoning.

## Ingredients
* 1kg Skinless Chicken Thigh Fillets
* 1 Chilli (the red ones)
* 50g Brown Sugar (tightly packed into a measuring cup)
* 1/3 cup ginger
* 2 Shallots *or* 1/2 Red Onion
* 45ml Fish Sauce
* 45ml Vegetable Oil
* 125ml *boiling* water
* *optionally* Coriander or Red Chilli to garnish:
## Instructions
1. Cut the chicken into 5cm chunks, then throw them in a bowl.
2. Deseed and finely mince the chilli, put to one side, then finely julienne[^2] the ginger to roughly 5cm pieces.
3. Add the chilli to the bowl, and additionally add the fish sauce. Toss the chicken to coat (you can use your hand if it's easier). Leave to one side to marinate.
4. Add the oil and sugar to a large non-stick pan, then heat over a medium-high heat. The moment the sugar has melted, remove the pan from the stove, then **⚠️ carefully** (it'll sizzle!) add the chicken from the bowl.
5. Add the ginger and shallots (or onion) to the pan, and give everything a brief toss or mix.
	* The caramel might harden as you're working this - that's okay, it'll melt again as we get going.
6. Put the pan back on the hob, and stir just until the chicken has sealed[^1] - don't brown it, or cook it through.
7. Add the water (making sure it's hot when you do!), and bring to a simmer while stirring. Once achieved, simmer rapidly for 10 to 12 minutes until the liquid reduces down to a glaze. This might take longer if your pan is smaller or your stove isn't as strong - that's okay!
	* Stir every now and then while it's watery to help it along.
8. Once it's reduced down to a glaze, toss regularly to get the colour on the chicken. The further into glaze you keep cooking it, the better the colour will turn out!
9. Once happy, serve with some kind of white rice, and optionally garnish with the coriander and chilli.

Leftovers should be safe for 2-3 days in a sealed Tupperware or similar.

[^1]: "Sealing" chicken means to get it to the point where it's turned white all over. This isn't the point where it's cooked, and it's definitely not safe to eat at this point, but the outside shouldn't have any pink rawness left.
[^2]: Julienning means to make long batons - think like really thin baguettes.
