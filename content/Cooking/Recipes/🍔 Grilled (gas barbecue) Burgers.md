*This recipe has been built up over the years from a few Youtube videos.*

> [!info]
> Unless you really know what you're doing, and where your mince has come from (not just the supermarket!), it's safest to cook to well done (70 celsius).

> [!warning]
> Always wash your hands after handling raw meat, along with any surfaces it touches - unless you enjoy food poisoning.

## Equipment
* Gas Barbecue (I don't have much experience with charcoal, so this recipe may not work properly)
* (optionally) Meat Thermometer (the faster the read, the better - see [Equipment](../Equipment))
## Ingredients
* 150g Mince (per burger)
* Salt
* Pepper
* *optional* American Cheese slice, if you're after a Cheeseburger
	* These are sometimes called *Cheese Singles*. They come in a pack of individually wrapped sheets.
## Instructions
1. Pull 150g of mince out of your pack, weighing it out to be just right.
2. With your hands, form the mince into a reasonably tight meatball, then slowly start to turn it into a hockey puck. You're aiming for something a little wider than the buns you're serving them into, as they'll shrink during cooking.
3. Put a small divot into the middle of the burger with your thumb - the middle will expand a little as you cook, and you don't want to be left with a domed burger.
4. Leave to settle, and preheat your barbecue with the lid closed to around 200c for about 10 minutes.
5. Once you are ready to start, generously salt and pepper both sides of the burger (not too much!), then add the tiniest amount of oil to one side (less than a teaspoon's worth) to prevent sticking.
	- You can also choose to oil the barbecue rack directly, but be careful - it'll be pretty hot!
6. Place the oiled side of the burger straight onto the rack, close the lid, and get a comfy chair.
7. You'll want to check and flip the burgers every 4 minutes or so - don't be tempted to flip before 4 minutes, otherwise it will probably stick to the rack, potentially destroying it in the process. It should release naturally with time, so if it doesn't want to go, keep checking it every minute or so.
8. Make sure to keep the lid closed to get an oven effect going, which will make sure the inside of your burgers stays relatively moist and juicy.
9. After flipping twice, check the internal temperature of the burger with your thermometer. You're looking for a temperature above 70c for well done.
	* If you don't have a thermometer, keep an eye out for clear running juices - once you've hit this point, it's a good idea to cut one open and make sure it's fully cooked through.
10. *optional* Before serving, toast your buns inside-down for a minute or two - it can make all the difference!
11. *optional* 30 seconds before removing from the heat, place your American cheese slice over the top of the burger to give it a bit of time to melt.
12. Remove from grill, place on top of burger, serve with condiments of your choosing!


### Troubleshooting
* My burger's really dry, what happened?
	* Chances are that you cooked it a bit too long, or with the lid open. Check a bit more regularly with a thermometer.
