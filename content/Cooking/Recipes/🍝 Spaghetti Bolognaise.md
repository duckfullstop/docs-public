#recipe #recipe/basic/beginners

*This recipe was supplied by Kat, who prepared it for me on countless occasions. Thanks, dork.*

> [!warning]
> Always wash your hands after handling raw meat, along with any surfaces it touches - unless you enjoy food poisoning.

## Ingredients
* 500g Mince
* 2 decently-sized Onions
* 1 can of Chopped Tomato
	* This usually comes in a can with sauce.
* 1 box of Passata
	* You can usually find this next to the Chopped Tomatoes. You want the straightforward one with nothing else (some come with extra herbs or garlic).
	* If you can't find this, add another can of Chopped Tomatoes.
* 2-4 cloves of garlic
	* You can use squeezy garlic or similar, but you may find the end result a bit too acidic. Fresh is best.
	* Adjust to suit your taste.
* Italian Herbs (jar)
	* You can usually find this as store-brand mixed herbs. Check the jars isle.
	* *Sainsbury's* (UK supermarket) sell it.
* 2 OXO Cubes (or stock pots to taste)
* *Optionally* Mushrooms
* 2-3 Tablespoons Sugar *to taste*

## Instructions
1. Start by browning the mince in a pan with some olive oil
2. Whilst browning, dice the onions and mushrooms (if you’re having them)
3. Once the mince is browned, transfer to a bowl or plate temporarily
4. Add a little more olive oil to the pan, then add the onion and mushroom
5. Cook until the onions are translucent, or the mushrooms have released enough water
6. Mince or crush the garlic (while you wait for the above!)
7. Once the vegetables have cooked, add the mince back to the pan, along with the garlic - but try to fry the garlic separately from the rest
8. After 1 minute of frying the garlic, add the stock cubes / stock pots and mix to combine
9. Add the chopped tomatoes / passata, along with a decent shake of italian herbs and some sugar
10. Mix together, cover, and simmer for at least 5 minutes.
11. Grab a small spoon, and have a slurp (*let it cool down on the spoon first, don't burn your mouth!*). This is your opportunity to add stuff to your taste - try adding more herbs, or perhaps an oxo cube or two - or maybe some salt and pepper. It's very hard to ruin it unless you go berserk - you'll be learning every time you experiment!
	* If you find the sauce is too thin, you can take the lid off and start boiling it for a while to get rid of some of the water - keep a very close eye on it though, and stir regularly to avoid burning, but keep in mind that *the sauce will thicken when it cools*.
12. Once you've got it vaguely where you like it, cover and simmer as slowly[^1] as possible. You'll find that the longer you let it sit, the more the flavour will develop and get even better. Maybe don't leave it more than a few hours though, and definitely come back and stir it from time to time (usually every 15-30 minutes).

You may need to adjust the herbs, sugar, and stock to taste - this is a recipe of feeling, not quantities.


[^1]: "Fast" and "Slow" refers to how quickly you're simmering / boiling something. In this case, get the heat as low as possible to get some *slow* bubbling, but not so low that everything stops.
