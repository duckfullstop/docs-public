## Travel
The national rail operator is Nederlandse Spoorwegen (_NS_), although some rail and metro services are operated by private companies such as _GVB_ and _R-Net_.

The good news is that there is an integrated payment system, called _OV Chipkaart / OV Pay_. If you're just visiting for a short while, then _OV Pay_ is the way to go, and just uses normal credit and debit cards / mobile payments.

If you're staying longer, or plan on taking multiple longer journeys, it might be worth your time and effort to get an _OV Chipkaart_. There are two variants:

* Anonymous: Can be purchased from any ticket machine for a €5 deposit, then topped up. Needs €20 minimum credit to start an NS journey, less for metro / bus. **Cannot be loaded with discount tickets.**
* Personal: Must be purchased in advance, either with a form from a ticket office or online for a €7 deposit. Online delivery only to Dutch, Belgian, or German addresses. Needs a portrait photo for ID.

The Personal chipkaart is probably the one you want, but you're going to need to find a friend to get it delivered to. However, if you can figure that one out, you can then sign up for _NS Flex_, which unlocks **a lot** of options.
### NS Flex
_Flex_ is a postpay product that can be attached to any **personal** Chipkaart. With it active, you get access to the following:

* Monthly postpayment of all fares from your bank account
* _OV Fiets_ - Super cheap (€4.50 per day) bike rental from stations.
* Park & Ride / bike parking integrated billing
* **Season tickets** for various discounts (analogous to the UK's _Railcards_, but better)

The difficulty (and the reason this guide was written) is that signing up for _Flex_ with a foreign IBAN is notoriously difficult. If you try to proceed like normal with _Mijn NS_ (the online portal), you'll find two problems:

1. The address entry fields will only accept a Dutch address.
2. If you can get past that, payment must be done with iDeal, which absolutely requires a Dutch bank account.

The work around is to contact customer service, and you have a few options to do so:
* The easiest way if you're not socially anxious is to call NS Customer Service ([088 672 11 05](tel:+31307515155) - note international calling charges apply, use a VOIP service if you can) - Pick Option 5 for english, then option 6 for Flex - you may find you get a voicemail box if you call outside Mon-Fri 9-4:30. 
	* Explain to them that you have a personal Chipkaart, and that you have a foreign IBAN but wish to sign up for Flex. You should be asked for the chipkaart number, your email address, billing address, and bank details (IBAN, BIC number, and bank name), as well as which season ticket you want. _Note there is a €10 administration fee, which is waived if you subscribe to a season ticket._
* You can also contact them through ~~X~~ Twitter or Facebook, and they're equally helpful there. Responses during the day are usually within an hour.

Once that's done, and you're registered on _Mijn NS_, you should see new, exciting options for changing travel class, etc.

**You will need to collect the Flex entitlement from a ticket machine before your first journey. This should be explained to you when contacting customer service.**

> [!warning]
> Please be warned that while NS can attach any IBAN to their billing system, it sometimes explodes horrifically when they try to actually bill you later on, **especially with UK accounts**. If this happens, you'll get an email from them telling you to pay up, along with their IBAN and a reference number so you can settle the invoice manually.

#### Season Tickets
Season tickets come in varying flavours, ranging from a 20% discount only at weekends, to unlimited national travel. They are all paid monthly, but **require a chipkaart with NS Flex active**.

You can find a full list here: https://www.ns.nl/nsflex/webshop

##### The Combined Travel Discount
If you have any season ticket, up to 4 people travelling with you also get access to the same discount. Here's how to do it:

* **Do NOT turn on your own combined travel discount toggle** - this is for if _you're_ travelling with _someone else_ with the discount.
* If the other travellers have personal Chipkaarts with Mijn NS:
	* Simply have them toggle the discount on, reminding them to toggle it off if they're not travelling with you.
* If the other travellers have anonymous Chipkaarts:
	* Head to an NS ticket machine, scan the Chipkaart, then select the "Combined Travel Discount" option to apply it. Remember that the reverse will need to be done when they're finished.
* In all other cases, buy either an e-Ticket or a single use Chipkaart with the Combined Travel Discount enabled. Note that single use Chipkaarts have a €1 extra fee associated with them.

Ticket inspectors can tell which Chipkaarts have the discount enabled, so don't think you can get away with leaving it on.

The combined travel discount **cannot be used with OV Pay.**  Use an e-Ticket or a single use Chipkaart.

#### OV Fiets
OV Fiets is a bike rental service provided by NS. It's available at all major stations, along with an increasing number of smaller ones. _It requires Flex_.

Rental costs €4.85 a day, and you can rent up to 2 bikes per Chipkaart. Rental ends when you return the bike to the station.

To rent an OV Fiets, simply place your Chipkaart on the reader on the rear rack of the bike, and it should unlock automagically.

##### A note for people that don't know what a Dutch bike is
OV Fiets are of the Dutch style - that is, **they do not have traditional brake handles.** To slow down, _pedal backwards_ - you'll feel resistance, that's the brakes.
I'd strongly suggest you cycle around for a bit first before going out on the roads to get used to it, but once you've got it figured out you'll wonder why more countries don't do the same thing!
