# Important Information
When an alternative OS is installed on your system, we have two options.
## Fresh Installation
With a fresh installation, we completely erase all information on your system **irrecoverably**. This means that anything on your system will be removed, and you'll have a completely new installation of your chosen Alternative OS. This is the solution we generally recommend, as it is usually the most stable.

**Having a backup for a Fresh Installation is absolutely essential**, and we will not proceed without clear reassurance that you have one - and confirmation that it works. Once your new OS is installed, you can simply copy across all of your existing personal files from your backup.

One excellent option for a backup when performing a fresh installation that we can recommend is for you to **purchase a replacement Solid-State Drive** for your system, that we will replace your existing Hard Drive or Solid-State drive with, and additionally an **external caddy** to place your existing drive in. This way, you have the flexibility of being able to quickly access your existing files as you please, but also the option to simply swap the existing drive out if you need to use your old Windows 10 installation for any reason. One of our repairers will walk you through what you would need to purchase for this solution if it applies to you.

## Dual-Boot
With a Dual-Boot installation, we will shrink the size of your Windows installation to make room for your Alternative OS, and then install the Alternative OS alongside it. When you turn your computer on, you will be given a menu that lets you pick whether to boot either Windows or your Alternative OS. Once you are comfortable with your Alternative OS, and have recovered all of your files, you have the option to completely remove your existing Windows 10 installation.

We generally don't do this as a matter of routine for a couple of reasons: first, if Windows performs certain kinds of updates, it can break the boot menu, requiring technical knowledge to recover from. Second, while it is possible to resize each installation down the line, it can be difficult - especially if your Windows installation is already at maximum capacity.

With the above being said, it can make sense in a few scenarios - one of our repairers will talk you through it if we believe it's a good option for you.

**We still strongly encourage a working backup for Dual-Boot installations.**


# About Your Uses

As part of the process of making sure this upgrade goes as smoothly as possible for you, we ask you to go away and have a think about the things you do with your system - both frequently and rarely!

In particular, we need to know about any devices or hardware you use, such as printers, scanners, and headphones (not exhaustive!), as well as Windows-specific software, such as Computer-Aided Design or Games.

Between now and the next café, please write down the following on this sheet:
### Devices
* Make, model, and function of any devices you may plug in or interface with
	* Examples: "Canon MX925, Printer / Scanner", or "Janome Sewing Machine"

### Software
* Name and function of any software installed and still needed
	* Examples: "Google Chrome, Web Browser", "Steam, Gaming Software", or "Microsoft Money, Money Management Software"

### Games
* Name and platform of any games you play or are likely to play
	* For example, "Overwatch 2, Battle.net", "Farming Simulator 2022, Steam"

Being as complete and thorough as possible will help reduce any teething issues or unexpected incompatibilities. **Thanks for your help!**
