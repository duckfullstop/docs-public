You've likely been given this sheet because you need to know how to complete a backup of your Windows system before work can be completed.

## What is a Backup?

Let's start with the basics: A _Backup_ is a 1 to 1 copy of all of the files on your system. This can include important system files, but always includes your personal data.

Backups are extremely important for one very simple reason: if any of the data on your system is lost, be that through either catastrophic failure of your hard drive / solid state drive, corruption, ransomware attack, reinstallation of the operating system, or some other issue, a backup will let you easily recover your information. **Without a backup, there is often absolutely no way to restore your files.**

You can store a backup in plenty of places. Common solutions include:
* Cloud storage, such as Microsoft OneDrive, Google Drive, or Apple's iCloud. This can be a good option for your personal files, but if you have a lot, then it can be quite pricy. Windows heavily encourages you to backup your data to OneDrive.
* External USB storage, such as a USB hard drive or USB stick. Generally a good option, as you can unplug the storage and keep it to one side when you're not doing your backup.
* Network Attached Storage ('NAS'). This is a small computer on your home network that stores your data, and your computer simply synchronises with it on a regular basis when you're at home.
